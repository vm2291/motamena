Programs · Cycles · Schedule

One area at a time, taught well.

Launched in September 2024, the Academy completed its first year with a weeklong onsite intensive at NYU Abu Dhabi in June 2025. Since September 2025, its second year has moved through four cycles of olympiad mathematics. Each cycle closes with teacher presentations.

Format

One coach, one cycle, one or two related areas.

A guest coach leads each cycle through one or two related areas. Live online sessions come with full written notes, and participant presentations close the cycle.

Online live sessions

Approximately biweekly weekend sessions, each led by a guest coach and hosted on the NYU Abu Dhabi Zoom platform. Sessions are normally scheduled on Friday, Saturday, or Sunday in the late afternoon, subject to coach availability and regional time differences.

  • Opening and orientation (~10 min)
  • Lecture and worked exposition (~80 min)
  • Break (~15 min)
  • Collaborative problem solving (~75 min)
  • Accompanying LaTeX notes for every session

Year One capstone (June 2025)

Year One concluded with a weeklong onsite intensive at NYU Abu Dhabi in June 2025, a single capstone bringing together the year's participants and coaches.

  • 16 to 20 June 2025, A6 Building, NYU Abu Dhabi
  • Daily lectures and problem solving sessions with guest coaches
  • Hosted by SITE at NYU Abu Dhabi

Anatomy of a session

What a live session looks like.

Every cycle is built around live online sessions. The most recent one, Cycle 4 · Session 1, 24 April 2026, is a useful example.

Cycle 4 · Session 1, Advanced Number Theory

Topic: Exponential congruences, Fermat's Little Theorem, Euler's Theorem and multiplicative orders.

Live date: 24 April 2026 · NYU Abu Dhabi Zoom platform.

Cycle coach & live mathematical exposition: Prof. Dušan Djukić, University of Belgrade and Serbian Mathematical Society.

Pedagogical development, mathematical expansion and LaTeX preparation: Dr. Dania Zantout, Academic Program Architect & Director.

This session opens the advanced number theory strand. Participants develop the following skills:

  1. State and prove Fermat's Little Theorem using the residue permutation method.
  2. State Euler's Theorem and compute Euler's totient function from a prime factorization.
  3. Use small moduli, 4, 7 and 9, to force parity, divisibility and contradictions in exponential Diophantine equations.
  4. Define multiplicative order and explain why ord_n(a) divides φ(n) when gcd(a, n) = 1.
  5. Translate divisibility conditions such as 2a−1 | 2b−1 into conditions on exponents.
  6. Apply the smallest prime divisor method in problems involving n | 2n ± 1.
  7. Recognise how a short congruence argument can prepare the ground for the lifting the exponent lemma.
The mathematical arc is Fermat → Euler → cycles of powers → multiplicative order → olympiad applications. The pedagogical arc is to begin with familiar small cycles and then reveal that those cycles are governed by a general structure.
