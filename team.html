Team

The people behind the Academy.

The Academy was developed with funding from SITE, which was part of the NYU Abu Dhabi Research Institute, and in academic partnership with MIMS, the Mediterranean Institute for the Mathematical Sciences. The inaugural year of the Math Olympiad Trainer Academy received additional support from the Office of the Provost at NYU Abu Dhabi. Its cycles are led by invited olympiad mathematicians and educators drawn from the wider academic network. Several invited coaches are individually affiliated with the Mathematical Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts (SANU). These affiliations identify each coach's home institution. SITE and MIMS hold the Academy's defined institutional roles.

Academy Leadership & Founding Team

Prof. Nader Masmoudi

Founding Chair

Professor of Mathematics, Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences (NYU), affiliated faculty at NYU Abu Dhabi, and Founder and Principal Investigator of SITE, which was part of the NYU Abu Dhabi Research Institute. A 1992 International Mathematical Olympiad gold medallist, the first African and first Arab contestant to win IMO gold, he later placed first in the joint entrance competition of the École Normale Supérieure and École Polytechnique (1994). His distinctions include the Republic of Tunisia's academic prize (1994), the Annales Henri Poincaré best scientific article distinction (2011), an NSF fellowship (2012), Senior Clay Math Scholar recognition (2014), a Chair of Excellence from the Fondation Sciences Mathématiques de Paris (2016 to 2017), the Fermat Prize (2017), an invited address at the International Congress of Mathematicians (2018), the Kuwait Prize (2019), election to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (2021), and the King Faisal Prize in Science for Mathematics (2022).

Prof. Sadok Kallel

Founder · Strategic Liaison

Prof. Sadok Kallel is a Professor of Mathematics at the American University of Sharjah and a research fellow at the Laboratoire Paul Painlevé at the University of Lille. He holds a PhD in Mathematics from Stanford University. His research spans algebraic and differential topology, homotopy theory, configuration spaces, and their applications to geometry and analysis. He is a founding member of the Mediterranean Institute for the Mathematical Sciences, which he directed from 2012 to 2022 and now serves as Chairman Emeritus. Sadok Kallel represented Tunisia at the 1985 International Mathematical Olympiad, where he earned a bronze medal. He was the chairman of the Tunisian committee for the Olympiad from 2019 to 2021.

Dr. Dania Zantout

Founder · Academic Director & Program Lead

Dr. Dania Zantout is a mathematician, an award-winning mathematics educator and leader in responsible, educator-led AI. Building on a university teaching career that began in 2001, her work spans curriculum and program development, assessment design, educational research, instructional technology, mentorship, and institutional service. At NYU Abu Dhabi, she coordinates multi-section mathematics courses and develops evidence-based systems that make students’ invisible thinking visible by tracing how they reason, confront misconceptions, revise, and transfer learning. Her work in responsible AI advances pedagogically grounded systems that preserve educator authority, student reasoning and authorship, authentic assessment, and institutional data sovereignty. Dr. Zantout founded and leads WiMN+AI and the Takāmul Cup. As a founder and director of the Math Olympiad Trainer Academy, she oversees its website, directs its pedagogical framework and curriculum structure, and created the Olympiad-to-Classroom Activity, a model for translating Olympiad problem-solving into classroom practice. Mentorship remains central to her work, from supporting graduate students in developing their teaching to mentoring undergraduate researchers through the REU program. Her teaching has been recognized with three awards at Clemson University and the inaugural NYU Abu Dhabi Palm Teaching Award. In 2026, she was shortlisted for Most Innovative Teacher of the Year in the Times Higher Education Awards Arab World. Dr. Zantout holds a PhD in Mathematical Sciences from Clemson University, where her research focused on automorphic forms and their L-functions. She also holds a BS in mathematics, with a thesis in cryptography, and an MS in pure mathematics, with a thesis in complex analysis. Her central commitment is to expand educational opportunities while maintaining mathematical rigor, academic standards, and the essential role of human judgment.

Dr. Zineb Hassainia

Founding Program Coordinator

Dr. Zineb Hassainia is a mathematician and Ramón y Cajal Research Fellow in the Department of Applied Mathematics at the University of Granada. Her research lies at the intersection of nonlinear analysis, partial differential equations, and mathematical fluid dynamics, with a particular focus on vortex dynamics, the Euler equations, active scalar models, free-boundary problems, and KAM theory. Dr. Hassainia earned her PhD in Mathematics from Université de Rennes in 2015. She held a postdoctoral appointment as a Courant Instructor at New York University’s Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences. She subsequently joined New York University Abu Dhabi as a Research Scientist, where she further developed her research program in nonlinear analysis and mathematical fluid dynamics. She later joined the University of Granada as a Ramón y Cajal Research Fellow after placing first in Mathematics in the 2023 Ramón y Cajal call, a highly competitive distinction awarded within Spain’s “Attraction of Talent” program. Her work has led to significant advances in the analysis of rotating, periodic, and quasi-periodic vortex structures, contributing to a deeper understanding of coherent structures, stability, and long-term dynamics in fluid systems. Her research has appeared in leading international peer-reviewed mathematics journals, including Inventiones Mathematicae, Communications on Pure and Applied Mathematics, Memoirs of the American Mathematical Society, and the Journal of the European Mathematical Society.

Cycle Coaches

Each cycle of the Academy is led by an invited guest coach. Coaches are olympiad mathematicians and educators with significant experience training national teams.

Dr. Melih Üçer

Cycle 1 Coach · Year Two (2025 to 2026)

Postdoctoral fellow at KAUST (Saudi Arabia), working on partial differential equations and, specifically, mean field games. PhD from Bilkent University (Turkey) in geometric topology. An active olympiad trainer for over ten years, both in his native Turkey and in several countries across the region. Led Year Two's opening cycle across four weekend sessions, Olympiad Geometry (14 and 28 September 2025) and Olympiad Number Theory (12 and 26 October 2025), with full LaTeX problem sets on angle chasing, Miquel's lemma, divisors and gcd identities, and exponential Diophantine problems.

Prof. Dušan Djukić

Cycle 4 Coach · Year Two (2025 to 2026)

University of Belgrade and the Mathematical Institute SANU. A longstanding figure in the Serbian olympiad tradition. Closed out Year Two with three live sessions on advanced number theory, 24 April, 9 May, and 24 May 2026, covering Fermat's little theorem, Euler's theorem, multiplicative orders, the Lifting the Exponent lemma, and primitive roots.

Đorđe Baralić

Coach & SANU Coordinator

Research Associate Professor at the Mathematical Institute SANU, Belgrade. PhD in algebraic topology from the University of Belgrade (2013); research in topology, combinatorics, geometry and discrete mathematics. Lecturer at the Mathematical Grammar School in Belgrade and at the Awesome Mathematics Summer Program. Bronze medalist at IMO 2004 and former team leader of Serbia at the Junior Balkan Mathematical Olympiad. Since 2017 a principal organizer of the May Month of Mathematics, Serbia's annual math festival; a passionate promoter of mathematics who has visited more than 65 countries.

Miloš Đorić

Coach · Geometry & Olympiad Training

Assistant Professor at the University of Belgrade, Faculty of Mathematics, Department of Geometry. PhD in Differential Geometry; his area of interest is the theory of submanifolds of Kähler and nearly Kähler manifolds. Teaches the honors class at the Mathematical Grammar School, a school with High National Distinction Status, and prepares students at the High School for Informatics for the national competitions in mathematics. Team leader of Serbia's national team for the Junior Balkan Mathematical Olympiad and president of the National Committee for Junior Competitions in Serbia. Member of the editorial board of Matematički list.

Stevan Gajović

Coach · Number Theory

Postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn; previously a postdoctoral researcher at Charles University, Prague. PhD under Steffen Müller at the University of Groningen. Research areas: number theory and computational arithmetic geometry. Prepared students of the Mathematical Grammar School for domestic and international competitions for several years, including the team for the European Girls Mathematical Olympiad, once first place, once third. Author of many competition problems; bronze and silver medalist at the IMO, with further medals at RMM, BMO and JBMO.

Zdravko Cvetkovski

Cycle 3 Coach · Year Two (2025 to 2026)

Mathematician from North Macedonia. Former Assistant Professor at European University, Skopje. Competition commission member and author of Inequalities, Theorems, Techniques and Selected Problems (Springer). Led Year Two's third cycle on the Cauchy Bunyakovsky Schwarz inequality across three live sessions (6 February, 13 February, and 27 March 2026), with the cohort's teacher presentation returning to his material on 10 April 2026.

Ilja Uzelac Bujišić

Cycle 2 Coach · Year Two (2025 to 2026)

Bachelor's and Master's from the University of Cambridge. IMO Bronze Medalist (Croatia). Research interests include probability, machine learning, and financial mathematics. Returned for Year Two's second cycle on combinatorial games and invariants, three live sessions on 28 November, 12 December, and 26 December 2025 covering invariants and monovariants, parity and coloring arguments, winning and losing positions, Nim, cellular automata, and Conway's Game of Life. Also jointly led the Year One onsite summer intensive at NYU Abu Dhabi in June 2025.
