Team

The people behind the Academy.

The Academy was developed with funding from SITE, which was part of the NYU Abu Dhabi Research Institute, and in academic partnership with MIMS, the Mediterranean Institute for the Mathematical Sciences. The inaugural year of the Math Olympiad Trainer Academy received additional support from the Office of the Provost at NYU Abu Dhabi. Its cycles are led by invited olympiad mathematicians and educators drawn from the wider academic network. Several invited coaches are individually affiliated with the Mathematical Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts (SANU). These affiliations identify each coach's home institution. SITE and MIMS hold the Academy's defined institutional roles.

Academy Leadership & Founding Team

Dr. Nader Masmoudi

Founding Chair

Professor of Mathematics, Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences (NYU), affiliated faculty at NYU Abu Dhabi, and Founder and Principal Investigator of SITE, which was part of the NYU Abu Dhabi Research Institute. A 1992 International Mathematical Olympiad gold medallist, the first African and first Arab contestant to win IMO gold, he later placed first in the joint entrance competition of the École Normale Supérieure and École Polytechnique (1994). His distinctions include the Republic of Tunisia's academic prize (1994), the Annales Henri Poincaré best scientific article distinction (2011), an NSF fellowship (2012), Senior Clay Math Scholar recognition (2014), a Chair of Excellence from the Fondation Sciences Mathématiques de Paris (2016 to 2017), the Fermat Prize (2017), an invited address at the International Congress of Mathematicians (2018), the Kuwait Prize (2019), election to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (2021), and the King Faisal Prize in Science for Mathematics (2022).

Dr. Sadok Kallel

Founder · Strategic Liaison

Dr. Sadok Kallel is a Professor of Mathematics at the American University of Sharjah and a research fellow at the Laboratoire Paul Painlevé at the University of Lille. He holds a PhD in Mathematics from Stanford University. His research spans algebraic and differential topology, homotopy theory, configuration spaces, and their applications to geometry and analysis. He is a founding member of the Mediterranean Institute for the Mathematical Sciences, which he directed from 2012 to 2022 and now serves as Chairman Emeritus. Sadok Kallel represented Tunisia at the 1985 International Mathematical Olympiad, where he earned a bronze medal. He was the chairman of the Tunisian committee for the Olympiad from 2019 to 2021.

Dr. Dania Zantout

Founder · Academic Director & Program Lead

Dr. Dania Zantout is a mathematician, an award-winning mathematics educator and leader in responsible, educator-led AI. Building on a university teaching career that began in 2001, her work spans curriculum and program development, assessment design, educational research, instructional technology, mentorship, and institutional service. At NYU Abu Dhabi, she coordinates multi-section mathematics courses and develops evidence-based systems that make students’ invisible thinking visible by tracing how they reason, confront misconceptions, revise, and transfer learning. Her work in responsible AI advances pedagogically grounded systems that preserve educator authority, student reasoning and authorship, authentic assessment, and institutional data sovereignty. Dr. Zantout founded and leads WiMN+AI and the Takāmul Cup. As a founder and director of the Math Olympiad Trainer Academy, she oversees its website, directs its pedagogical framework and curriculum structure, and created the Olympiad-to-Classroom Activity, a model for translating Olympiad problem-solving into classroom practice. Mentorship remains central to her work, from supporting graduate students in developing their teaching to mentoring undergraduate researchers through the REU program. Her teaching has been recognized with three awards at Clemson University and the inaugural NYU Abu Dhabi Palm Teaching Award. In 2026, she was shortlisted for Most Innovative Teacher of the Year in the Times Higher Education Awards Arab World. Dr. Zantout holds a PhD in Mathematical Sciences from Clemson University, where her research focused on automorphic forms and their L-functions. She also holds a BS in mathematics, with a thesis in cryptography, and an MS in pure mathematics, with a thesis in complex analysis. Her central commitment is to expand educational opportunities while maintaining mathematical rigor, academic standards, and the essential role of human judgment.

Dr. Zineb Hassainia

Founding Program Coordinator

Dr. Zineb Hassainia is a mathematician and Ramón y Cajal Research Fellow in the Department of Applied Mathematics at the University of Granada. Her research lies at the intersection of nonlinear analysis, partial differential equations, and mathematical fluid dynamics, with a particular focus on vortex dynamics, the Euler equations, active scalar models, free-boundary problems, and KAM theory. Dr. Hassainia earned her PhD in Mathematics from Université de Rennes in 2015. She held a postdoctoral appointment as a Courant Instructor at New York University’s Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences. She subsequently joined New York University Abu Dhabi as a Research Scientist, where she further developed her research program in nonlinear analysis and mathematical fluid dynamics. She later joined the University of Granada as a Ramón y Cajal Research Fellow after placing first in Mathematics in the 2023 Ramón y Cajal call, a highly competitive distinction awarded within Spain’s “Attraction of Talent” program. Her work has led to significant advances in the analysis of rotating, periodic, and quasi-periodic vortex structures, contributing to a deeper understanding of coherent structures, stability, and long-term dynamics in fluid systems. Her research has appeared in leading international peer-reviewed mathematics journals, including Inventiones Mathematicae, Communications on Pure and Applied Mathematics, Memoirs of the American Mathematical Society, and the Journal of the European Mathematical Society.

Cycle Coaches

Each cycle of the Academy is led by an invited guest coach. Coaches are olympiad mathematicians and educators with significant experience training national teams.

Dr. Miloš Đorić

An Assistant Professor at the University of Belgrade, Faculty of Mathematics, Department of Geometry. He has a phd in Differential Geometry and his area of interest is theory of submanifolds of Keahler and nearly Keahler manifolds. He works in Mathematical Grammar School, a school with High National Distinction Status, where he teaches specially selected students in the honors class. He also works in High School for informatics, a private high school for the students interested in programming and informatics, where he prepares students for the national competitions in mathematics. He is the team leader of the national team of Serbia for Junior Balkan Mathematical Olympiad and president of the National committee for junior competitions in Serbia. Miloš Đorić is a member of the Editorial board of "Matematički list", a mathematical magazine for elementary school students and teachers in Serbia.

Dr. Ilja Uzelac Bujišić

Completed his bachelor's and master's studies at the University of Cambridge between 2019 and 2023. He is currently pursuing a PhD in the mathematics of machine learning while being affiliated with the Mathematical Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts (MI SASA). His research interests span probability, machine learning, and financial mathematics. In addition to his academic work, he is a co-founder of an educational startup. Previously, he was an accomplished mathematical competitor, earning silver medals at the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) and the Balkan Mathematical Olympiad (BMO) in 2018. He is passionate about training gifted students for mathematical competitions, particularly international olympiads.

Dr. Đorđe Baralić

A Research Associate Professor at the Mathematical Institute SANU in Belgrade, SANU. He received his PhD in algebraic topology from the University of Belgrade in 2013. His research interests, in addition to topology, are combinatorics, geometry, and discrete mathematics. As a lecturer, he worked at the Mathematical Grammar School in Belgrade and the Awesome Mathematics Summer Program. He is a passionate traveller who has visited more than 65 countries and is a promoter of mathematics. Since 2017, he has been one of the main organizers of the May Month of Mathematics, the annual math festival in Serbia. He won the bronze medal at the IMO 2004 and was the team leader of Serbia at the Junior Balkan Mathematical Olympiad.

Dr. Stevan Gajović

A postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn. Previously, he was a postdoctoral researcher at Charles University Prague. He was a PhD student of Steffen Müller at University of Groningen. His research areas are number theory and computational arithmetic geometry. He prepared students of Mathematical Grammar School for domestic and international competitions for several years and in particular, the team for the European Girls Mathematical Olympiad which once won the first and once the third place. He was a member of the committee for mathematical competitions in Serbia and he was an author of many competition problems. He won one bronze and one silver medal at the IMO and several more medals at other international competitions: RMM, BMO, JBMO.

Dr. Melih Üçer

A postdoctoral fellow at KAUST (Saudi Arabia), working on partial differential equations and, specifically, mean field games. PhD from Bilkent University (Turkey) in geometric topology. An active olympiad trainer for over ten years, both in his native Turkey and in several countries across the region. Led Year Two's opening cycle across four weekend sessions, Olympiad Geometry (14 and 28 September 2025) and Olympiad Number Theory (12 and 26 October 2025), with full LaTeX problem sets on angle chasing, Miquel's lemma, divisors and gcd identities, and exponential Diophantine problems.

Dr. Dušan Djukić

An Associate Professor at the University of Belgrade, his primary academic research focuses on approximation theory and numerical analysis. Alongside his academic tenure, Professor Đukić is a highly distinguished and experienced Math Olympiad trainer. He brings an international perspective to our academy, having successfully coached the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) teams of Serbia, Saudi Arabia, Pakistan, Brazil, and Armenia.

Zdravko Cvetkovski

Graduated as a mathematician from the Math. Department, (4 years), at the State University of Ss. Cyril and Methodius, Skopje, Macedonia. His graduate work was Quadratic residues (Number Theory). After that, he has finished his master studies at the same department (2 years) with magisterial thesis Quadratic fields and their application, also in the field of Number Theory. He is currently pursuing his PhD studies at the same university. Until 2024, he was working at the European University, Skopje, Macedonia, a private university, as an assistant professor of mathematics for nearly 12 years. He taught courses at the Faculty of Informatics and the Faculty of Economics. During this period, he instructed various mathematics courses, including Calculus 1 and 2, Discrete mathematics, Algebraic structures, Linear algebra with Analytic geometry, Probability theory, Mathematics for economists and Statistics. He has dedicated a significant amount of time to participating in commissions for mathematical competitions in his country and organizing national competitions. For 10 to 12 years, he has been a member of committees responsible for proposing and selecting problems for mathematics competitions at regional and national levels, as well as for the Macedonian Mathematical Olympiad, where he contributed many of the problems. In addition, he has worked extensively with talented mathematics students from Macedonia, and in recent years, he has expanded his mentoring to international students from the USA, UK, Italy, and other countries, preparing them for their national competitions and the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO). For many years, he has participated in training national teams for international competitions. He has also served as the leader and deputy leader of his country’s junior teams on several occasions. Furthermore, he has created numerous original mathematics problems for various competitions and tests worldwide. As part of his expertise, he works on part-time projects as an editor and error checker for many well-known Math Olympiads around the world. He has published several articles in national magazines and, as coauthor, has contributed to several books on competitive nature in Macedonian language. He has also published one book “Inequalities- Theorems, techniques and selected problems” by Springer publishing house (http://www.springer.com/gp/book/9783642237911)
