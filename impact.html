Learning and mathematical method · L1 to L3

From named techniques to usable ways of thinking

In number theory choose as big as you can ... in i.equalities am gm how to indentify thé ves inequality... in séquences how to suppose thé solution exists and construct it ... in geometry angle chasing

P05 · Anonymous participant reflection

One idea I now use is the invariant method from Cycle II. It changed the way I approach and teach problems by encouraging me to look for what remains unchanged rather than immediately searching for a formula. For example, when teaching problem solving, I encourage students to identify patterns or properties that stay constant throughout a process, helping them develop deeper logical thinking and explore problems more creatively.
